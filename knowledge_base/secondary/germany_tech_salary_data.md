## 1. Why Germany for Tech and Business Careers

Germany is the largest economy in Europe and the third largest globally (by GDP). It hosts the European headquarters of major global tech firms (SAP, Siemens, BMW, Bosch, Deutsche Telekom, Zalando) alongside a dense network of Mittelstand companies — mid-sized industrial firms that are increasingly digitising their operations.

**Key labour market signals (2024–2025):**
- Germany faces a structural shortage of over **340,000 IT specialists** (Bitkom, 2024), with demand accelerating due to AI adoption across all industries
- AI and data-related roles have seen **salary growth of 12–18%** year-on-year since 2022
- English is widely accepted as a working language in tech, consulting, and international business roles — making Germany accessible for non-German speakers
- The EU AI Act (2024) is creating new compliance, ethics, and governance roles that require both technical and regulatory knowledge

## 4. Salary by City

Germany's tech salaries vary significantly by location. Munich and Frankfurt lead due to the density of large corporations and financial institutions; Berlin is the startup hub with slightly lower but rapidly growing compensation.

| City | Relative Premium vs. National Average |

Germany consistently ranks among the top 3 European countries for tech salaries, behind Switzerland and ahead of the Netherlands and UK (post-Brexit for non-UK nationals).

## 6. Graduate Employment & Return on Investment

- **Average time to first job** for SRH graduates in tech programs: cited as under 3 months by SRH career services data
- **Employment rate within 6 months** of graduation: 87–93% for applied sciences graduates in STEM fields (Bundesagentur für Arbeit, 2024)
- **Typical starting salary uplift** for graduates with embedded internships vs those without: +12–18% (StepStone, 2024)
- **MBA / Master's premium**: Holding a Master's degree adds an average of €8,000–€15,000 to starting salary in Germany versus Bachelor's-only roles
