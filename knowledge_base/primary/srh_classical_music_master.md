## Programme Overview

The Master's in Classical Music builds on a Bachelor's degree and offers a four-semester specialisation for advanced instrumentalists and vocalists. The programme develops technical and artistic skills at the highest level, preparing graduates for a professional career in the music world.

Core components include:
- Intensive study of your primary instrument or voice
- Advanced chamber music, orchestral playing, and solo performance
- Lieder, music theatre, and other vocal genres
- Music theory, music history, and analytical methods
- Individual artistic projects and research assignments
- Masterclasses, workshops, and regular public performances

---

## Specialisations

### 🎻 Focus: Instrumental
Develop your instrumental skills to the highest level, with your main instrument at the centre. You will refine technique and expression, find your own musical style, and study ensemble and chamber music, music theory, aural training, music history, and aesthetics. Alongside musical training, you build independence through your own artistic projects and expand your management skills.

### 🎤 Focus: Vocal
Develop your voice as your primary means of expression over four semesters. Intensive work on vocal technique, interpretation, and stage presence is complemented by ensemble and chamber music, speech training, sight-singing, scenic lessons, music theory, and music history. Practical experience through concerts, recitals, and music theatre productions is integral to the programme.

## Study Location

The programme is based at the **Institute for Classical Music and World Music**, part of the SRH University Campus Berlin in the heart of **Kreuzberg**. The campus offers modern music rooms, professional studios, and creative labs. The institute bridges classical music and trans-traditional styles, and is surrounded by a vibrant network of music studios, galleries, and cultural projects.

## Career Prospects

### Artistic Careers
- Orchestral musician (symphony or chamber orchestra)
- Soloist or chamber musician
- Opera singer or instrumentalist in opera houses and theatres
- Freelance concert artist at festivals and international series
- Studio or session musician
- Choir singer, song interpreter, church musician
- Artist in interdisciplinary, media, and music technology projects

### Education & Teaching
- Instrumental or vocal teacher at music schools, conservatories, or universities
- Lecturer in performance practice, musical interpretation, or music education
- Academic researcher (historical performance practice, vocal techniques, instrumental didactics)

### Cultural Management
- Artistic direction
- Concert and festival organisation
- Artist agency and project coordination
- Orchestra and opera house administration
- Music festival coordination or artist management

## Admission Requirements

- University degree (Bachelor's or equivalent, minimum 180 ECTS; music degree not mandatory)
- English language certificate
- Curriculum vitae with current passport photo
- Copy of ID / National Identity Card
- Entrance examination (audition)

International applicants must provide university entrance qualifications from prior education and successfully complete the selection procedure.

## Application Process

1. Submit online application via the SRH application portal
2. Receive invitation to the admissions interview (if requirements are met)
3. After successful interview, receive acceptance letter and study contract
4. Sign and upload the study contract
5. Reserve your place by paying the enrolment fee within the guarantee period
6. Receive your letter of admission
7. Apply for a study visa if required
8. Pay tuition fees as per your study contract
9. Participate in Welcome Week and begin your studies
